- Source: Adaptive Geometry / ARP Lyapunov program

- Description: Proved Lyapunov contraction theorem for ARP conductance dynamics under Kirchhoff current coupling. Upgrades the existing registry entry eq-arp-lyapunov-stability (score 66, placeholder template) to a complete theorem with numerical certificate. In the frozen-current case, the quadratic Lyapunov function V decays at exact rate -2 mu_G. Under full Kirchhoff-coupled dynamics where currents I_ij depend on conductances through the network Laplacian, the decay rate degrades to -2(mu_G - kappa) where kappa is the Kirchhoff Lipschitz constant. Contraction holds whenever mu_G > kappa. Numerical certificate: predicted decay rate -0.0500, measured -0.0500 (12-node random graph, dt=0.01, 2000 steps).
- Equation: V = \frac{1}{2\alpha_G}\sum_{ij}(G_{ij}-G^\star_{ij})^2,\quad \dot{V} = -2\mu_G V \;\text{(frozen-current)};\quad \dot{V} \le -2(\mu_G - \kappa)\,V \;\text{(Kirchhoff-coupled)}
- Assumptions: Conductance dynamics follow the standard ARP reinforce-decay law: dG_ij/dt = alpha_G |I_ij| - mu_G G_ij.; The Lyapunov function is V = (1/(2 alpha_G)) sum (G_ij - G*_ij)^2 with G*_ij = (alpha_G / mu_G)|I_ij| the frozen-current equilibrium.; In the frozen-current regime, currents I_ij are held constant; the decay dV/dt = -2 mu_G V is exact.; Under Kirchhoff coupling, currents satisfy I = L(G) s for a Kirchhoff Laplacian L depending on G and source vector s.; The Kirchhoff Lipschitz constant kappa := sup_{delta G} ||abs(I(G+delta_G,s)) - abs(I(G,s))|| / ||delta_G|| is finite for bounded networks.; Contraction requires mu_G > kappa; when violated, the Lyapunov bound becomes non-negative and stability is not guaranteed by this certificate.
- Evidence: Upgrades existing registry entry eq-arp-lyapunov-stability (score 66, placeholder template) to a proved theorem.; Frozen-current case: standard quadratic Lyapunov computation gives dV/dt = -2 mu_G V exactly, no approximation.; Kirchhoff-coupled case: chain rule plus Lipschitz bound on |I(G)| yields dV/dt <= -2(mu_G - kappa) V.; Numerical certificate on 12-node random graph (dt=0.01, 2000 steps): predicted decay rate = -2 * 0.025 = -0.0500, measured exponential decay rate = -0.0500, exact match to 4 significant figures.; Kirchhoff Lipschitz probe: kappa measured at approximately 0.012 for the test network, satisfying mu_G = 0.025 > kappa.; Builds on White Paper 01 (ARP/AIN) reinforce-decay conductance law and registry entries #1 (BZ conductance), #2 (entropy-gated), #11 (Phase Adler/RSJ).; Recovers the generic Lyapunov template V >= 0, dV <= -alpha V as a special case when kappa = 0.

### Kirchhoff Collapse-Identity Falsifier
- Submission ID: sub-2026-04-18-kirchhoff-collapse-identity-falsifier
- Name: Kirchhoff Collapse-Identity Falsifier
- Source: Adaptive Geometry / ARP Lyapunov program
- Units: OK
- Theory: PASS-WITH-ASSUMPTIONS
- Description: Negative result documenting that the frozen-current equilibrium identity |I_ij| = (mu_G / alpha_G) G_ij does not hold as an asymptotic identity under full Kirchhoff-coupled ARP dynamics. This forces a PASS-WITH-ASSUMPTIONS qualification on entries that rely on this proportionality.
- Equation: \frac{\lVert\,|I_{ij}| - (\mu_G/\alpha_G)\,G_{ij}\,\rVert}{\lVert I \rVert} \not\to 0 \quad \text{under honest Kirchhoff dynamics}
